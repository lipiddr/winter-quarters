Peter I. Mesick

Travel West with the Willard Richards Company in 1849. Listed are Peter I. Mesick age 44; Cynthia Flint Mesick age 49; Almira Mesick age 9.

In the 1850 U. S. Census, Peter I Mesheck age 47; Cynthia Mescheck age 52; Amira Mesheck age 12 and Hugh Tyler Mesheck are listed as living in the Utah Territory.

On the 1870 U. S. Census, they are inhabitants of the Weber River Valley, Weber, Utah. Listed are Peter Mesick age 66; Cynthia age 72; Elizabeth age 50; Flavilla age 44 and John P age 10 months.

On the 1880 U. S. Census, the family is listed in West Weber Precinct, Weber, Utah. Listed are Peter age 76; Cynthis age 82; Elizabeth age 62; Flavilla age 54; Catharine age 12; and P. John age 10. Living next to them is Ammon Green age 47 and Almira Mesick Green age 41 and their children: Ammon age 21; Cynthia age 19; P. Henry age 17; George age 15; William age 13; Albert age 11; A Mary age 9; Edward age 7; Harriet age 6; Charles age 4; and Benjamin age 1.

William Ludlow McIntyre

He graduated from the Medical College in Physics and Surgery in 1838 and received his diploma from the College of Physicians and Sugeons in New York. He was assigned as an assistant surgeon for the Mormon Battalion.

Went West with the Daniel Spencer/Ira Eldredge Company in 1847. Listed are William Ludlow McIntyre age 35; Rosannah Brown McIntyre age 37; James N. McIntyre age 12; William Francis McIntyre age 6.

Robert Pixton

Was a Private in Company E of the Mormon Battalion.

Traveled to the Salt Lake Valley with the Jonathan H. Holmes/Samuel Thompson Company (1848).
Departure: 2 July 1848
Arrival: 6 September 1848

Company Information:
This company consisted of 44 men and 1 woman, 39 of whom were returning Mormon Battalion veterans. In early May a small survey group set out from Sutter's Fort to find a trail across the Sierra Mountains that could be traversed by wagons. This company constructed a wagon road over Carson Pass which was later used by many traveling to and from California. They left Pleasant Valley (near Placerville) and crossed the Sierras to Carson Valley. At that point the company divided into smaller groups and traveled to Salt Lake City.

On the 1850 U. S. Census, the family is living in the Great Salt Lake area, Utah Territory. Listed are: Robert age 32; Elizabeth age 31; Charlotte age 11; Elizabeth age 8; Robert age 6; James age 3; Mary Ann age 1.

On the 1860 U. S. Census, the family is listed as living in West Jordan, Salt Lake, Utah. Listed are Robert age 41; Elizabeth age 40; Robert age 10; Elizabeth age 7; Willard age 6; Joseph age 1.

On the 1870 U. S. Census, the family was still in West Jordan. Listed are Robert age 52 ; Elizabeth age 50; Robert age 20; Willard age 16; Joseph age 12; Sarai age 8.

On the 1880 U. S. Census the Precinct is listed as North Jordan. Listed are Robert age 60; Elizabeth age 50; Joseph age 21; and Sariah age 17.

According to Passenger and Immigration Lists and the Overland Trail, it looks as if Robert returned to the United States in 1865 and cam West with the Henson Walker Company in 1865.

Pictures of their tombstones are on Findagrave.com
