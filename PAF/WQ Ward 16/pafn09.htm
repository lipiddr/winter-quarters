Winter Quarters Ward 16 Residential Area

Notes

Henrietta Keyes

Henrietta Keyes Whitney age 25 and Samuel Alonzo Whitney age 6 came West with the Abraham O. Smoot-George B. Wallace Company in 1847. The company departed on 18 June 1847 and arrived on 25 September 1847. There were 223 individuals in the company and it began its journey from the outfitting post on the Elkhorn River about 27 miles west of Winter Quarters.

On the 1850 U. S. Census, Henrietta age 29 was listed with the James Hendricks family in Great Salt Lake, Utah Territory.

Samuel Alonzo Whitney

On the 1850 U. S. Census, Samuel, age 10, was living with the Ira I Willis family.

Don Carlos Whitney

Buried in Grave #14. Died of unknown causes.

George Taylor

This is the only male Taylor that died in Nauvoo. It may not have been the husband of Lovisa or Louisa Curtis or the father of William H. Taylor.

Louisa or Lovisa Curtis

Louisa or Lovisa may have married a Mr. Smith between 1846 and 1854. She did have a child by the name of Samuel W. Smith that was born 1848. No records have been found.
